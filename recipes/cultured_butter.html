Cultured Butter

Description

Rich and nutritious, cultured butter develops the cream usually used for butter with a culture of buttermilk for up to a week in advance,
allowing natural processes to develop deeper flavor and texture. Once mixed, the buttermilk can be saved to use for future batches.

Time Investment

35 minutes active : 5 minutes prep, 1-7 days culture, 30 minutes mix

Ingredients

  • Tools: Stand mixer,rubber spatula, cheesecloth, storage container
  • Heavy cream, as much as you like
  • 1/4 cup of cultured buttermilk
  • Kosher salt
  • Ice cubes

Steps

  1. Pour your heavy cream into the bowl of a stand mixer and add your buttermilk culture.
    Stir slightly, cover loosely with plastic wrap, and let sit for up to a week.
    The longer it sits, the stronger the flavor it will develop.
  2. When you're ready to make your butter, place the bowl back into the stand mixer and beat it
    on medium speed with a whisk attachment. As the fats begin to cluster, the butter will separate from the buttermilk.
  3. When it fully separates, prepare the cheesecloth in a bowl and begin pouring the mixture out,
    letting the cheesecloth hold back the solids. Set aside any of the buttermilk that you wish to keep,
    discard or use the rest
  4. Gently squeeze the cheesecloth around the butter solids, squeezing out large pockets of fluid
  5. Turn the cheesecloth inside out and gather the solids into a large mixing bowl
  6. Fill the bowl with cold water and begin kneading and smearing the butter in the bowl with the rubber spatula
    Add ice cubes to keep the butter solid in the water during this process
  7. As you smear and knead the butter, it will release small pockets of buttermilk into the water.
    As the water becomes cloudy, pour it out and refill the bowl. Repeat as needed until kneading the butter
    leaves the water totally clear.
  8. Pour out all of the water and knead the butter to release the clean water left behind.
    Water left in the butter will reduce its shelf life.Add flakes of salt during this process
    to spread them through the butter and improve the flavor.
  9. Pack it into containers and store it in the refrigerator. The flavor will continue to develop
    while the butter stays at room temperature.
